---
title: "How does Bright SDK impact my answers to the LG privacy questionnaire?"
description: "Starting August 2024, LG requires developers to fill in a privacy questionnaire for their apps (same practices used in other app stores e.g. AppStore, Amazon, and Google Play)."
sidebarTitle: "LG privacy questionnaire"
---

It is important to note that Bright SDK does not collect any personal information that can identify or track your users. However, it collects the IP address and performs some crash/performance analytics. Therefore, when you submit an app to the LG Appstore with Bright SDK, in addition to any privacy answers you provide in the questionnaire, please use the following:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Privacy Policy" titleSize="h3">
    **Fill in your privacy policy link**
    
    - Data collected within the app → `YES`.

    <img
      src="/integration-guides/image/LG_1.png"
      alt="Alt text"
      width="400"
    />
  </Step>

  <Step title="Device ID" titleSize="h3">
    **Analytics**
    
    - Identifiers: Device ID → `Analytics`.

    <img
      src="/integration-guides/image/LG_2.png"
      alt="Alt text"
      width="400"
    />
  </Step>

  <Step title="Other Identifier" titleSize="h3">
    **Analytics**
    
    - Location of saved data.

    (Please select the country where the collected data is stored. If the country is not listed in the country list, or if the privacy policy specifies this information, choose to refer to the privacy policy and enter the link to the privacy policy.)

    <img
      src="/integration-guides/image/LG_3.png"
      alt="Alt text"
      width="400"
    />
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select the country with saved data" titleSize="h3">
    - Select the country with saved data → `U.S.A`.

    <img
      src="/integration-guides/image/LG_4.png"
      alt="Alt text"
      width="400"
    />
  </Step>

  <Step title="Shared Data" titleSize="h3">
    - Identifiers: `Device ID` & `Other Identifier`.

    <img
      src="/integration-guides/image/LG_5.JPG"
      alt="Alt text"
      width="400"
    />
  </Step>
</Steps>
